🧾 Election Day Guide

Everything you need to know for a smooth, confident voting experience on Election Day.

🗓️ 2026 General Election: Tuesday, November 3, 2026. Polls open 7:00 AM – 8:00 PM statewide.

Before You Go

1

Confirm Your Polling Place

Look up your polling location. It may have changed since last election.

2

Grab Your ID & Voter Card

Bring your RI photo ID and your voter card if you have one. Get a voter card.

3

Research the Ballot

Know who and what you’re voting for before you arrive. Check your sample ballot at vote.ri.gov.

4

Plan Your Travel

Allow extra time if taking public transit. RIPTA offers free rides to the polls on Election Day.

At the Polls

5

Check In

Give your name and address to the poll worker. You’ll sign the poll book and receive your ballot.

6

Mark Your Ballot

Use a pen to fill in the oval next to your choices. Take your time — there’s no rush.

7

Feed Your Ballot into the Scanner

Insert your ballot into the tabulator. If it’s rejected, a poll worker will help you request a new ballot.

8

Get Your Sticker!

Pick up your “I Voted” sticker and share it on social with #RIVoted.

Your Rights at the Polls

SituationYour Right
You’re not on the voter rollRequest a provisional ballot — it will be counted after your eligibility is verified
You need assistanceYou may bring a helper of your choice (not your employer or union rep)
You’re in line at 8 PMYou must be allowed to vote — stay in line!
You’re told you can’t voteCall the RI Board of Elections: (401) 222-2345
