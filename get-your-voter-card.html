What is a Voter Card?

A Rhode Island Voter Card is your official proof of voter registration. It shows your name, address, voting district, and polling place. You may present it on Election Day for faster check-in.

How to Get Yours

1

Verify Your Registration

Make sure you are registered to vote in Rhode Island. If not, register here first.

2

Submit Your Request

[Form will go here] — Enter your name, address, and date of birth to pull up your voter record.

3

Receive Your Card

Your card will be mailed within 7-10 business days, or pick up at your local Board of Canvassers.

4

Bring It on Election Day

Present your card at your polling place for quick, easy check-in. Find your polling place →

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the voter card required to vote?
No. Rhode Island law does not require a voter card to vote. However, it speeds up the check-in process and serves as official proof of your registration.
How long does it take to receive my card?
Typically 7-10 business days by mail. Expedited pickup is available at your local Board of Canvassers.
What if my address has changed?
Update your registration first, then request a new card. Update registration →
